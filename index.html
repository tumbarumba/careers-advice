Why Do We Go To Work?

  • We have to. Money, debts, survival. We're inherently lazy. Extrinsic motivation
  • We love to. Being part of something, building something we care about. We're inherently motivated if all the problems will just get out of the way. Intrinsic motivation

Drive

  • Autonomy
  • Mastery
  • Purpose

To motivate employees who work beyond basic tasks, give them these three factors to increase performance and satisfaction:

  • Autonomy — Our desire to be self directed. It increases engagement over compliance.
  • Mastery — The urge to get better skills.
  • Purpose — The desire to do something that has meaning and is important. Businesses that only focus on profits without valuing purpose will end up with poor customer service and unhappy employees.

The GROW Model

  • Goal: where do you want to be?
  • Reality: where are you now?
  • Obstacles: what's stopping you?
    Options: how are you going to deal with the obstacles?
  • Way forward: convert the options to action steps
  • This can be done individually, or with a mentor

Advice to Juniors

  • Learn to build the thing right
  • Find a mentor
  • Improve your communication skills
  • Technical skills. Languages. Libraries. APIs. Techniques. XP
  • Mentors can help you practice your skills and work towards your goals
  • Communication skills give you power and influence

Advice to Seniors

  • Learn to build the right thing
  • Become a teacher
  • Find a mentor
  • Improve your communication skills
  • Understanding customer needs. Feedback loops
  • Mentors can help you practice your skills and work towards your goals
  • Teaching others improves your own skills
  • Communication skills give you power and influence

Advice to Managers

  • Learn to influence culture
  • Find a mentor
  • Improve your communication skills
  • You don't work in code any more, you work in people. People is culture. Complex adaptive systems.
  • Managers have an oversized influence on culture (access to metacultural forces)
  • Not a manager, still learn this stuff
  • Coursera course: Culture Driven Team Building
  • Story of changing culture: Turn The Ship Around, by David Marquet
